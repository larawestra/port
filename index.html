§ 02

Specialisms

Why these threeI chose these for different reasons. Security drew me in through the theory. Organised violence is deeply embedded in international politics, and across history the same logic keeps recurring. Its effects are disproportionate, wherein a single actin one place can move alliances and markets far from where it happened. Emerging technology started with an aesthetic. I was drawn to speculative worlds, to the games, music, and design that build the future before it exists. These technologies are borderless by design, while the institutions governing them are organised by borders, and that gap is what makes them analytically interesting. Regulation is, in a way, the opposite. Rules are written down, dated, and enforceable, and they commit a state or an entity to a position. The judgement has already been made about who it trusts and what it wants to protect. Knowing those rules is what keeps you ahead of the risk.

Scaling Dynamics: Regulatory Risks in Global Equity Incentive Structures

This brief examines how compensation design in fast-scaling company's like Hurdle can create misaligned incentives, driving behaviour that conflicts with regulatory expectations and internal risk appetite. The analysis maps key risk pathways from incentive structure to conduct outcome, identifying where the greatest vulnerabilities sit and what strategic adjustments would bring compensation design into better alignment with governance and compliance frameworks.

May 2026

Base Metals Tariffs: Political Risks for Chinese Supply Chain Exposure

Apollo Association was facing direct exposure to Chinese trade tariffs across their merchandise supply chain, with escalating US-China trade tensions creating urgent uncertainty around sourcing costs, customs risk, and supplier relationships. This brief delivered a situational analysis of the tariff landscape as it applied to their specific business, mapping the key political and regulatory risks alongside a stakeholder analysis of the actors shaping trade policy. The output gave the client a clear picture of their exposure and a strategic framework for navigating it, whether through supplier diversification, pricing adjustments, or regulatory positioning.

China–UK · Supply chain
Apr 2026

Japanese Market Entry: Political Risks for Investment

Odrysia Capital was evaluating a Japanese market entry amid one of the most uncertain macroeconomic environments in decades, rising interest rate volatility, yen instability, and compounding geopolitical pressures created significant gaps in their existing risk coverage. This brief mapped those exposures across monetary policy and FX risk, identifying where the portfolio was most vulnerable and where strategic opportunity remained viable. The output gave the client a forward-looking risk-adjusted view of their Japan prospects, actionable intelligence rather than a general market overview.
